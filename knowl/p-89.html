\begin{equation*} r=\sqrt{\frac{250}{\pi}} \approx 8.92 \end{equation*}
To the left of that \(r\text{,}\) \(C'(r)\) isn’t 0, so it’s either positive or negative and we can easily evaluate \(C'(r)\) at some point to the left and see that it is negative. So, \(C(r)\) decreases until it reaches \(r \approx 8.92\text{.}\) Similarly, to the right of that \(r\text{,}\) we see that \(C'(r)\) is positive so \(C(r)\) increases. Thus, by first derivative analysis, we see that \(C(r)\) is minimal at \(r \approx 8.92\text{.}\)
