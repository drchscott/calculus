So, what do we think the slope of the tangent line is likely to be? It’s apparent that if we let \(h\) continue to shrink, the associated lines have slopes that are getting closer and closer to \(2\text{.}\) Plotting the line through \((1,1)\) with slope \(2\) gives us exactly the tangent line depicted in black. So, at least for the function \(f(x)=x^2\) and the point \((1,1)\) on its graph, we’ve solved the tangent line problem!
