In general, it is apparent that if the graph of some function, say \(f(x)\text{,}\) is above the graph of some other function, say \(g(x)\text{,}\) over an interval of \(x\text{,}\) say \([a,b]\) then the area trapped between them can be calculated as
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{lll} \int_a^b f(x)dx - \int_a^b g(x)dx \amp = \amp \int_a^b (f(x)-g(x))dx \end{array} \end{equation*}
